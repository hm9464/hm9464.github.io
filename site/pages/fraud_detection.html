Payment Fraud Detection

Jupter notebooks for all data loading, parsing, and analysis can be found here

Background

This is my submission for the IEEE-CIS Kaggle Competition to predict the likelihood of fraud in an electronic payment (credit/debit cards).

1. Exploratory Data Analysis

We want to get a better understanding of the data elements before diving into model building to gain some intiution first.

Transaction Amounts

One of the key data elements is the amounts of individual transactions. We will start by simply looking at the distribution frequency of transaction amounts.

Product Types

There are 5 product types, each with different transaction amounts. This signals that the product type may have a relation to the cost of the item.

The above plot looks a little too clustered. We can sort of derive that C is a cheaper product, but thats it.


Looking at the mean and standard deviations of these products, product 'W' has the highest mean ($122.40) and standard deviation ($126.12), while product C has the lowest mean ($42.87) and standard deviation ($38.92). I think this could be a very useful feature for the model as I would expect higher priced items to be more susceptible to fraud.

Card Attributes

Card attributes include provider (American Express, Discover, Visa, MasterCard). It is possible that certain providers are more likely to be fraudulent, perhaps due to different security standards.

Card Provider Transactions Fraud Pct Fraud
American Express 8225 239 2.91%
Discover 6171 436 7.06%
MasterCard 186045 6396 3.44%
Visa 378463 13013 3.43%

So it seems like Discover has a much higher probability of fraud compared to the other card providers. This will be helpful as we build the model, as the provider type is likely to have some predicitve power.

Now lets look at credit and debit cards more generally.

This plot illustrates the probability of fraud for different transaction amounts. Interestingly the majority of fraud actually occurs for lower value items, which goes against my initial hypothesis. Credit cards seem to also have higher proportions of fraud.
